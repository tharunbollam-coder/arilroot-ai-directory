promptTechniques:
  - title: "First-and-Last Frame Mapping"
    concept: "Relying strictly on text descriptions to explain a complex, moving transition can cause the video to warp, jitter, or introduce strange digital glitches mid-clip."
    workflow: "Instead of using only text, take advantage of the platform's frame-matching slots. Upload a clear initial image to establish your starting scene composition, upload a final image to define how you want the scene to finish, and use a connective text prompt inside your query block such as: 'A slow cinematic tracking push forward across the terrain, smoothly morphing and transitioning from the first-frame structure into the final-frame configuration.' This completely anchors the visual layout from beginning to end."
  
  - title: "The Chronological Sound Timestamp"
    concept: "Because the tool renders multi-layered audio right along with the video tracking, failing to tie sound events to specific visual actions can cause background noises to play completely out of sync."
    workflow: "Bind your visual descriptions directly to explicit timing landmarks inside your prompt box. Use a structured framework wrapped in quotation marks like this: 'Visual: At the 3-second mark, a heavy iron vault door aggressively slams shut. Audio: A loud, synchronized, low-frequency metallic boom with a deep hollow echo that accurately reflects the room's physical dimensions.' This forces the generated sound waves to snap perfectly onto the corresponding video frames."

howItWorks: |
  The mechanics are designed to copy how a real-world camera lens and recording crew function. When you feed it a text description or a photo, the system evaluates real-world physics rules, calculates accurate lighting trajectories, and keeps character details stable frame by frame so movements look natural. While it compiles the visual footage, a dedicated audio engine contextually calculates three distinct layers of clean sound—clear spoken dialogue matched to mouth movements, realistic physical collision noises, and steady environmental background atmospheres—layering them directly onto your video timeline automatically.
