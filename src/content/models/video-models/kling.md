    concept: "Normally, generating a video using AI means making one single 5-second shot at a time, downloading them all, and spending hours cutting and trimming them inside an editing app to make a story."
    workflow: "You can skip the editing room entirely by writing a full sequence out using simple brackets directly inside your text prompt box. When you set it up like this, Kling reads the entire script structure and outputs the changing camera angles and clean cuts natively inside a single video file."

  - title: "The Character Consistency Workflow"
    concept: "Trying to make the exact same character appear in multiple different clips with the same face, clothing, and hairstyle is usually a massive headache that requires advanced editing skills."
    workflow: "To lock down your subject's look, open the asset library and upload up to four clear photos of your character from a few different angles. For the best results, use reference photos where the character is standing against a plain, solid white background so the AI can easily isolate them. Once uploaded, keep the core descriptions of your character uniform in every text prompt you write, and Kling will seamlessly map that identical face and outfit across entirely new settings and actions."

  The process is completely straightforward: you type out a description of what you want to see, drop in an optional reference photo, and let the model build the scene from scratch.
